Project Overview

The Scaling Partnerships and External Collaborations through Industry-Aligned Learning (SPECIAL) project enhances participating institutions’ capacity to build and sustain workforce-focused external partnerships across emerging technologies. EPIIC funding supports staff capacity, partnership-building spaces, technical assets that improve partnership processes, and cohort-wide collaboration through retreats and special events.

Intellectual Merit

SPECIAL advances knowledge and practice for how emerging research institutions can engage key technology sectors to promote economic growth, STEM competitiveness, and national security—tracking metrics such as partners, projects, participants, events, and feedback.

Broader Impacts

SPECIAL strengthens workforce readiness by building internal capacity (training, professional development, infrastructure) and external capacity (industry/community partnerships) to align education with regional and national priorities, supporting sustainable talent pipelines in areas like manufacturing and technology.

Consortium members

Five institutions, diverse regions and strengths, aligned around shared goals: expanding external partnerships, strengthening internal infrastructure, and enhancing community and industry engagement.

Estrella Mountain Community College (EMCC)

Avondale / Phoenix area, Arizona

  • Expand educator-industry externships (Industry Immersion).
  • Enhance Microsoft Data Center operations with part-time staffing and iMasons collaboration.
  • Host expos and establish advisory boards for AIT and cybersecurity.

Houston Community College (HCC)

Houston, Texas

  • Create an AI Ecosystem Manager role to coordinate partnerships (Archimedes Initiative).
  • Deliver regular industry engagement, tours, and field trips.
  • Leverage partnerships to enable internships, workshops, donations, and AI faculty recruitment.

SUNY Geneseo

Geneseo / Rochester area, New York

  • Launch Project ExPanD team for sustainable partnership infrastructure.
  • Implement CRM for partnership coordination and retention of institutional knowledge.
  • Create a Faculty Fellows program tied to external partner activities.

SUNY Polytechnic Institute (SUNY Poly)

Utica / Mohawk Valley, New York

  • Develop pilot satellite sites near industry clusters (e.g., Griffiss).
  • Adopt a partnership-focused CRM and conduct trainings.
  • Pursue national consortia membership (e.g., semiconductor-focused networks).

University of Wisconsin – La Crosse (UWL)

La Crosse, Wisconsin

  • Create faculty fellows support and training for community engagement.
  • Develop sustainable partnership policies and improve CRM integration.
  • Host annual collaboration events to align emerging tech needs (e.g., applied AI).
